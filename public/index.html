About Us

Rotaract Club of NITK is one of the five exclusive clubs of the institute as well as the sole socio-cultural club. Having its accreditation from Rotary International, established in 1962, Rotaract Clubs (or “Rotary in Action” as they called it) are commensurate with international standards. The term “Rotaract” is the culmination of the words “Rotary” and “Interact”. Rotary is an international, secular organization formed for the purpose of bringing together business and professional leaders in order to provide humanitarian services, encourage high ethical standards in all vocations, and help build goodwill and peace in the world. Rotaract Clubs worldwide are sponsored and supported by Rotary International. Rotaract originally began as a Rotary International youth program in 1968 at Charlotte North Rotary Club in Charlotte, North Carolina, USA, and has grown into a major Rotary-sponsored organization of over 9,539 clubs spread around the world and 219,397 members. It is a service, leadership and community service organization for young men and women between the ages 18–30.

The club conducts various events over multi-faceted disciplines - Social, Cultural, Arts, Media and Literature - and has therefore established itself as the only club to be conducting events across all domains for students and club members alike. Some of the events conducted last year were Headstart, Game of Thrones, JAM, Bluff master, Blockbuster, Rubik’s cube workshop, sign language workshop, Raazmataz, Fragfest, etc.

In 2015, Rotaract NITK conducted the first marathon run at NITK with the "NITK Beach Run" along with the help of the NITK Student Council and Alumni Association

Every year Rotaract Club of NITK, with the other four exclusive clubs of the institute i.e. IEEE, CSI, IE and ISTE conduct a Campus Clean Up Drive. The amount of waste collected last year was around one ton.

Keeping Rotary International’s motto of “Service Above Self” in mind, Rotaract Club, NITK Chapter aims at the development of young adults as leaders in their communities and workplaces. For the purpose the club has brought up SIG’s or Special Interest Groups like Tronics SIG and Comps SIG for those inclined towards the technical field, Literary and Arts SIG for the readers, Finance SIG, etc. with their own weekly schedules. Similarly Rotalks is a platform for the speakers, the philosophers and the debaters to let their heart out.

A cloth collection drive followed by an orphanage trip every year is merely an attempt by the club to ensure some facilities to those kids.

Moreover, the Annual Charity Play every year conducted by the club not only collects a good amount of charity but also showcases the talent of the students. Last year’s play, an adaptation of Rick Abbot's “Play On” collected an incredible amount of money which was put towards charitable causes.

The club also takes part in international service projects, in a global effort to bring peace and international understanding to the world. Needless to say, Rotaract Club is one of the very few clubs known and recognized globally. “Whispering Winds” is a monthly newsletter initiated by Rotaract Clubs of Kathmandu, Karachi, Bhutan and NITK together to present to each other our cultures, exchange views and opinions.

Rotutor is an initiative by Rotaract Club, NITK Chapter, a weekly program to provide Basic English knowledge to school kids at the NITK English Medium School. Whereas, Avanti is a mentoring program for students belonging to the lower middle class section of the society preparing for competitive engineering entrance exams. Besides the student mentors get an internship to add to their resume.

Putting in efforts for the purpose of the club, members of Rotaract Club, NITK till date have been able to present a myriad of events and activities, all mesmerizing and all focusing on the development of fellow students and the community.

Avanti

The NITK Chapter of Avanti Fellows has well moved onto achieving great results since its inception in the institute in 2013. It involves students from NITK across all branches and years, who volunteer to mentor 11th and 12th standard JEE aspirant students of JNV Mudipu. Every Sunday, mentors from NITK engage students of both the classes for roughly a three hour session, which includes basic doubt solving and concept revision; peer learning; and most importantly, psychological motivation of the students by the mentors.
Avanti has had major success regarding admissions into IITs and NITs. With respect to NITK, Chetan Gudisagar, an alumnus from JNV Pondicherry, secured the IT branch in NITK in 2013. In 2015, two students from JNV Mudipu, Preethan and Rathan joined NITK in the Civil Engineering and Computer Science branches respectively.
Avanti is a socially conscious enterprise touching and transforming the lives of thousands of students nation wide. Rotaract - NITK Chapter is proud to support the cause of quality education for all.

Ro Tutor

“In learning you will teach, and in teaching you will learn.”

The fancy match of the words Rotaract plus tutor and we got Rotutor. This program focuses on teaching English to 5th, 6th and 7th grade middle schoolers at the resident Kannada medium school at NITK to help them prepare for the real world. It focuses on teaching kids basic English grammar through songs, stories and well planned out lessons to increase English proficiency. Rotutor provides the kids with textbooks, notebooks and dictionaries. The sessions, held twice a week, focus on giving a basic understanding of English grammar to the students, through well planned out lessons, stories and active class participation through song recitals, to increase their linguistic proficiency.The joy of meeting the kids outside the school premises and conversing with them in English gives us positive feedback on out attempts and gives us the motive to participate more in their growth

OOperai

Ooperai is a philanthropic organisation based in Panama.They intend to study the impact of architecture and surroundings on the creativity of children in the 6-12 age group.
They've done projects in Italy, Panama and India in cities like Mumbai and Chennai. They conduct workshops that involves the awareness of the space/place through a clear lecture of the built environment, i.e. the value of the context and their primal typologies (Market, Church and Dwellings). These themes are approached through drawings, models and conversations.Operai's users belong to cities that have radically assimilated modernity, this is why OOperai's aim is to question their learned habits. We at Rotaract support this initiative through our orphanage trips. An NITK alumna, Rtr. Hari Krishnan was one of the project coordinator Of Ooperai in India. We planning on working on more projects this year in collaboration with the Ooperai team.

NITK Beach Run

Rotaract Club NITK, in conjunction with The Alumni Association, recently held the NITK Beach Run, which was a three-category marathon. The event consisted of the 7.5km, the 5km and the 2.5km runs, and each participant was given T-shirts and refreshments, with cash prizes as well for the winners. This event was the first of its kind to be held in this college. The marathons were undeniably a success, with more than a thousand registered participants running the various routes.

Beach Clean-Up Drive

Beach clean up drive is conducted to bring awareness among us students about how important it is to protect the pride of our college. Having a private beach for the college is great but maintaining it clean and not spoiling its natural environment is the responsibility that comes along with it. We conduct the beach cleanup drive in the odd semester every year in collaboration with the NITK Students' Council and the Coast Guard. Members of the club with other volunteers endeavour to clean up the trash along the coast of Arabian Sea that comes under NITK beach. It is a very satisfying experience to see the beach back in its pristine condition at the end of the drive. Every year, the Coast guard inaugurates the Beach clean-up drive.

All the necessary equipment for the cleanup including garbage bags, gloves and caps are given to the volunteers and at the end, the volunteers are provided with refreshments. Certificates are given away to the volunteers for their contribution.

Ro-Cares

Rotaract Club NITK chapter visits an orphanage every year. It is open for all Rotaract members as well as Non Rotaract members. A cloth collection drive is organised in NITK hostels so that old and unused clothes can be donated to inmates of the orphanage. During our visit there, we provide them lunch and clothes collected from the drive. We also give them stationery items and chocolates. On the last orphanage trip we took all the children to zoo. They were divided into 6 groups, and each group was assisted by 3 people from NITK. They enjoyed a lot. For many of them it was their first time when they had visited a zoo, so they were amazed to see the wildlife and the setting of the habitat at the zoo. Snacks was provided after the zoo visit and various games were played. We at Rotaract NITK aim to make the most of this day, tap inot the kid's imagination, and most of all give them a fun and frolic filled day!

Literary SIG

The Literary SIG (Formerly called The Book Club) is one of the Special Interest groups (SIG) of Rotaract NITK which was started in the academic year 2013-2014. It is part of the literary initiative undertaken by the club. Here members of the club who are passionate about reading books get together and choose a book to read for a period of one month. The meetings happen twice a month, giving the members a platform to express their views regarding the book- the plot, the characters, language etc. The discussions range from how the fictional stories are about life passages, with everyone having a different journey, to how each person involved in the discussion relates and interprets it in a different manner. The books that were discussed include classics like The Godfather, The Great Gatsby, Malgudi Days, To Kill a Mockingbird, Catcher in the Rye, etc. The club gives us a brilliant opportunity to follow our passion of reading and interact with others who share the same love for books.

Arts and Media

The Arts and Media SIG (formerly called Design and Media) is a special interest group that aims to help members bring out their creativity to a canvas. Members are pushed towards activating their creative side through mediums such as Adobe Photoshop(R) as well as Web and Poster Design.

Services

Being brought in for the first time in 2016, the Services SIG gives the opportunity for fellow members to make a difference in the social sphere of life. One of the most important initiatives that comes under this interest group is 'Rotutor'. Every member is expected to be play their role actively.
